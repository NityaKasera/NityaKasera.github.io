Publications

Alternative Energy and Storage: 2021 - 2025

The paper offers a comprehensive exploration of transitioning from finite fossil fuels to sustainable alternatives, underscoring the importance of renewable energy. It covers diverse sources like bioenergy, nuclear, and the crucial role of biotechnology. The focus extends to bioremediation, including hydrogen energy and LNG, contributing to environmental preservation. The core argument advocates a seamless shift from fossil fuels to replenishable resources. However, it acknowledges the necessity for further research into ongoing developments in nature-derived energy, emphasizing the urgency of adopting renewable solutions and the potential of biotechnological advancements for a cleaner, sustainable energy future.



Extraction of Silica from Agricultural Waste

The paper focuses on addressing India's significant pollution issue arising from the burning of agricultural waste, particularly rice husk. It introduces a novel approach of converting rice husk into nano-silica material, offering a solution to both waste disposal and environmental concerns. The study outlines the process of preparing pure nano-silica through an alkali extraction method with acid preparation, overcoming impurities found in rice husk. The potential economic impact is highlighted, suggesting that efficient utilization of rice husk could boost the Indian economy and foster rural development. The findings underscore the transformative potential of converting agricultural byproducts into valuable resources, paving the way for a new industrial sector in India.
